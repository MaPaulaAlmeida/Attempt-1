PART I

Beginnings

I spent my early years in Quito, Ecuador, surrounded by volcanoes and the warmth of family. My days were filled with curiosity—sketching on any scrap of paper I could find and inventing stories about the world around me. Those simple, imaginative moments became the foundation for my creative passions, even before I knew what they were future.

PART I

Becoming

As a teenager, I began exploring who I was and what I wanted to become. I was drawn to photography, drawing, and storytelling, slowly realizing how much I loved bringing ideas to life. Yet, I also faced moments of doubt, unsure if these passions could truly shape my future. It was a time of learning, not just in school, but about myself.

PART I

Transitions

Graduating from high school in Quito marked the end of one chapter and the start of another. I was excited, but uncertainty lingered, and the fear of the unknown crept in. What lay ahead felt unclear, but deep down, I knew I wanted to grow beyond my comfort zone and embrace whatever challenges came next.

PART I

New Paths

Moving from Quito to Porto, Portugal, was a leap into the unknown, filled with both excitement and fear. Learning a new language, navigating a different culture, and being far from home tested me in ways I never imagined. Yet, these challenges pushed me to grow, teaching me resilience and revealing opportunities I never thought possible.

PART I

Now & Back Then

Now, I’m studying Audiovisual Communication and Multimedia, immersing myself in creativity and growth. Each project refines my skills and brings me closer to the life I envision. With every step, I’m learning to embrace the balance between uncertainty and opportunity, building a future that feels meaningful.
